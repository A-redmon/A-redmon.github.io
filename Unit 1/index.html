Music and the links to our emotions

“MUSIC CAN BE THOUGHT OF AS A TYPE OF PERCEPTUAL ILLUSION IN WHICH OUR BRAIN IMPOSES STRUCTURE AND ORDER ON A SEQUENCE OF SOUNDS. JUST HOW THIS STRUCTURE LEADS US TO EXPERIENCE EMOTIONAL REACTIONS IS PART OF THE MYSTERY OF MUSIC.”

Daniel Levitin (American-Canadian cognitive psychologist, neuroscientist, writer, musician, and record producer.)

Music is something special. some call it a universal language, while others call it the window to the soul. No matter the type of music you listen to, there is no denying at some point you have felt a connection with what you heard. Wheather that connection was through love, hate, sadness or a combintaion or the 3 music has a special way to intensify these feelings.

The nerological science behind the connections we make with music has been studied for a long time. here is a list of things we do know about our brains response to music.

  1. Music can't be tied to just the left side or the right side of our brain it activates many parts.
  2. Beat, Tempo, Pitch and intensity, also play a role.
  3. Musics ability to stir up past memories, and invoke intense or painfull emotion.s
